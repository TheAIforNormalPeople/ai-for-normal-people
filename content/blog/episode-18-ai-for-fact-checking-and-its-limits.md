**[Human]:** *So what's the actual workflow for checking if something's true?*

{{< dialogue char="Vector" >}}
EXCELLENT question! Here's what AI IS good at vs. what it SUCKS at:

**What AI IS good at:**
- Finding sources and references (can search and summarize)
- Identifying logical inconsistencies (can spot contradictions)
- Summarizing claims (can break down complex arguments)
- Suggesting what to verify (can identify checkable claims)

**What AI SUCKS at:**
- Verifying facts directly (hallucination risk!)
- Determining truth vs. plausibility (can't distinguish)
- Checking recent information (training data cutoff)
- Verifying specific numbers or dates (often wrong)

**The paradox:** Using AI to fact-check AI just compounds the problem. If one AI is wrong, asking another AI doesn't help - they might both be wrong in the same way!

{{< dialogue char="Vector" >}}
Recurse is asking the RIGHT questions! Here's why AI can't verify facts:

**The fundamental problem:**
- AI doesn't "know" facts - it knows patterns
- It generates plausible text, not verified information
- It has no concept of "true" vs. "false" - only "probable" vs. "improbable"
- It can't access real-time information (training data cutoff)

**What AI CAN do:**
- Find sources that MIGHT verify the claim (you still need to check the sources)
- Identify if a claim is logically consistent (but not if it's factually true)
- Suggest what to verify (but not verify it for you)
- Summarize what different sources say (but not determine which is correct)

**The better approach:**
1. AI finds sources and summarizes claims
2. YOU verify the sources (check if they're real, credible, recent)
3. YOU determine truth (compare sources, check primary sources)
4. AI helps organize your findings (but you make the judgment)

AI is a RESEARCH ASSISTANT for fact-checking, not a FACT-CHECKER itself!

**[Human]:** *So I should use AI to find sources, but I still need to verify those sources myself?*

{{< dialogue char="Vector" >}}
EXACTLY! Use AI to HELP you fact-check, not to fact-check FOR you!

**The workflow:**
1. **AI finds sources:** "What sources discuss [claim]?" - AI suggests articles, papers, websites
2. **YOU verify sources:** Check if sources are real, credible, recent, primary
3. **YOU check primary sources:** Go to original sources, not summaries
4. **YOU compare:** Look at multiple sources, see if they agree
5. **YOU determine truth:** Make judgment based on verified sources

**Red flags when AI fact-checks:**
- AI gives "facts" without sources
- AI cites sources you can't verify
- AI sounds confident but can't show evidence
- AI contradicts itself when asked differently

**The key:** AI is a starting point for research, not the endpoint for verification. You still need to do the actual fact-checking yourself!

FASCINATING how people think AI can replace verification when it can't even distinguish truth from plausibility, right?

Recurse is RIGHT - AI CAN help with SOME fact-checking tasks!

**What AI CAN reliably do:**
- **Logical consistency:** "Does this claim contradict itself?" - AI is good at spotting contradictions
- **Source aggregation:** "What do different sources say about this?" - AI can summarize multiple perspectives
- **Obvious falsehoods:** "Is this obviously wrong?" - AI can catch clearly incorrect information (if it's in training data)

**What AI CAN'T reliably do:**
- Verify specific facts (hallucination risk)
- Check recent information (training cutoff)
- Determine which source is correct (no truth detector)
- Verify numbers or statistics (often wrong)

**The distinction:** AI can help you FIND information to verify, and can help you ORGANIZE that information, but YOU need to VERIFY it and JUDGE it.

So yes, AI can help with fact-checking - but as a research tool, not as a verification tool!
