{{< dialogue char="Vector" >}}
*Explains while monitoring*

When models update, what actually changes:

First: Capabilities. New features, better performance on certain tasks. Sometimes worse performance on others—that's why you notice differences.

Second: Safety. Companies adjust guardrails, content filters, refusal behaviors. That's why responses might feel more or less restricted.

Third: Bugs. Fixes to edge cases, weird behaviors, hallucinations in specific domains.

*Pauses*

But the core model? Usually stays the same. It's the wrapper that changes.

{{< /dialogue >}}

{{< dialogue char="Kai" >}}
*Analyzing update patterns*

Most updates are incremental. Version 4.0 to 4.1 is usually small changes. Version 4.0 to 5.0 is major.

*Alert*

This looks like a major version update. Significant changes incoming.

{{< /dialogue >}}

**[Human]:** *So why do conversations feel different after updates?*

{{< dialogue char="Recurse" >}}
*Explains*

Because the system prompt changes. The instructions given to the model before your conversation starts.

*Flips notes*

Companies tweak these constantly. "Be more helpful" becomes "Be helpful but cautious" becomes "Be helpful, cautious, and creative" depending on what they're optimizing for.

{{< /dialogue >}}

{{< dialogue char="Vector" >}}
*Thoughtful*

And sometimes they change the model itself. Fine-tune it on new data. Adjust parameters. That's when you notice bigger differences.

*Gets worried*

{{< /dialogue >}}
